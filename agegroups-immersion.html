Pre-K Partial Immersion Spanish

Studies have shown that children who are exposed to two or more languages at an early age tend to perform better in school than their monolingual peers. The younger children are when exposed to new languages and sounds, the greater the benefits.

All pre-kindergarten age children at Kinderplatz benefit from 3.5 hours of Spanish immersion every other morning. Up to 40 pre-k students are divided into one of two pre-k “homerooms” – Monarchs or Monarcas. All children begin and end their day in English, in their assigned homeroom. Spanish immersion occurs in the Monarca classroom from 8:30 to noon every day – for one group of pre-k children or the other. Teachers keep track of the rotation schedule, which is available for parent viewing, as well. The routine and the schedule is the same for both pre-k rooms, and they share a common curriculum as well. The English-only classroom focuses on English readiness skills the children will need upon entering kindergarten, while the Spanish immersion component is a bonus add-on to the curriculum that increases the youngsters’ ability and excitement for learning in many domains.

Our Pre-K Partial Immersion Spanish program is taught by long-term, native-Spanish speaking staff members, from Mexico and Colombia (respectively). Our instructional Spanish lessons are taught by different Spanish speaking (non-native), long-term staff member(s).

PLEASE NOTE: The Pre-K Partial Immersion Spanish program is separate from (and not to be confused with) the instructional Spanish lessons the toddlers and preschoolers receive once per week during the school year. These weekly instructional Spanish lessons, though, are an excellent foundation as preparation for students’ eventual transition into our unique Pre-K program.
