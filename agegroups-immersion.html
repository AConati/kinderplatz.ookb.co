Pre-K Partial Immersion Spanish

Background

In 2012, due to overwhelming interest from families of children enrolled at Kinderplatz, we added a Spanish immersion component to our already highly successful Pre-Kindergarten Program. Studies have shown that children who are exposed to two or more languages at an early age tend to have greater problem-solving skills, and perform better in school than their monolingual peers. The younger children are when exposed to new languages and sounds, the greater the benefits. We recognize that most children attending the Pre-K Partial Immersion Spanish Program at Kinderplatz will most likely not move on to attend a Spanish immersion elementary school, but that has not deferred us from wanting to provide this unique and rewarding program for our students.

Having exposure to and/or learning another language at a young age makes it easier to learn any language (ie. a child who learns Spanish in preschool will have an advantage learning French or German, or any other language as well). Young children absorb other languages like sponges (acquiring language rather than learning it), and are able to hear and say new sounds with much greater ease than older children and adults. The older a person gets, the harder it is to “pick up” a new language, especially with any kind of native-like ability.

How the Program Works

Up to 40 pre-kindergarten age children benefit from programmed Spanish immersion each week at Kinderplatz, and spend time with the English-only teachers as well as the native Spanish-speaking teachers. The two pre-k homerooms share a common curriculum (as the team of pre-k teachers plans together to ensure that goals and objectives are being met simultaneously), and the program is modified and/or adapted as needed each school year to meet the particular developmental needs of the children enrolled. While children’s Spanish immersion time tends to focus more on dramatic play, and the English-only time lends itself to greater focus on academic kindergarten readiness, all Kinderplatz classrooms utilize play as the primary vehicle for learning and the development of social-emotional skill-building.

Our Pre-K Partial Immersion Spanish component is taught by long-term, native-Spanish speaking staff members, from Mexico and Colombia (respectively). The English-only pre-k teachers are also long-term, with years of experience preparing Kinderplatz students for entry into kindergarten, as well as overall success in life.

PLEASE NOTE: The Pre-K Partial Immersion Spanish program is separate from (and not to be confused with) the instructional Spanish lessons the toddlers and preschoolers receive once per week during the school year. Our instructional Spanish lessons are taught by different Spanish speaking (non-native), long-term staff member(s). These weekly instructional Spanish lessons, though, are an excellent foundation as preparation for students’ eventual transition into our unique Pre-K program.
