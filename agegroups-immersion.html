Spanish Immersion Program

Background

In 2012, due to overwhelming interest from families of children enrolled at Kinderplatz, we added a Spanish immersion component to our already highly successful Pre-Kindergarten Program. Studies have shown that children who are exposed to two or more languages at an early age tend to have greater problem-solving skills, and perform better in school than their monolingual peers. The younger children are when exposed to new languages and sounds, the greater the benefits. We recognize that some children attending the Spanish Immersion Program at Kinderplatz may not continue to a Spanish immersion elementary school, but that has not deterred us from wanting to provide this unique and rewarding program for our students.

As the program has grown and evolved, in 2014 Kinderplatz employed the assistance of renown writer and consultant, Angèle Sancho Passe, in developing a comprehensive language immersion approach that meets children’s needs in developmentally appropriate ways while preparing them for entrance into kindergarten.

Angèle Sancho Passe, M.A is a best-selling author and consultant in early childhood education. She has many years of experience as a teacher, school district administrator, university faculty, master trainer, and coach. Angèleis the 2012 recipient of the Evelyn House award for outstanding contribution to the field of early childhood education in Minnesota. She has served on the Governing Board of the National Association for the Education of Young Children (NAEYC). Please visit her website to read more about her and her literary works.

The older a person gets, the harder it is to learn a new language, especially with any kind of native-like ability. Having exposure to and/or learning another language at a young age makes it easier to learn any language (i.e. a child who learns Spanish in preschool will have an advantage learning French or German, or any other language as well), due to their greater understanding of sentence structure, culture and language in general. Young children acquire language more readily than adults, in a more native-like capacity, and are able to hear and say new sounds with much greater ease than older children and adults.

How the Program Works

The program has been made flexible enough to be adjusted as needed each school year for preschool and/or pre-kindergarten age children—so that programming and curriculum can be tailored to meet the unique needs of the children and families involved. Please contact the director for more information on program schedule and availability.

The Spanish Immersion Program is intended for children whose first language is NOT Spanish and who already have a good basic understanding and ability to use English (meeting age level language assessment criteria). Teachers collaborate with each other to meet the same goals and objectives through the center-wide use of The Creative Curriculum and Teaching Strategies GOLD® assessment system – in both the English and Spanish immersion classrooms. All Kinderplatz classrooms utilize play as the primary vehicle for learning and focus on the development of essential social-emotional skills.

The Kinderplatz Spanish Immersion program is taught by long-term, native-Spanish speaking staff members, from Mexico and Colombia (respectively).

PLEASE NOTE: The Spanish Immersion Program is separate from (and not to be confused with) the instructional Spanish lessons the toddlers and preschoolers receive weekly during the school year. Our instructional Spanish lessons are typically taught by different Spanish speaking long-term staff member(s).
